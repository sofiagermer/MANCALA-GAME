Pieces
The pieces are called seeds.
Visually representable as disks or ellipses.
They must be placed inside the cavities.
Beginning of the game
Storages are empty (no seeds)
The remaining cavities have the same number of seeds
Playstyle
Players sow their seeds alternately
In their turn they sow from one of their cavities
Depending on the situation of the last seed sown, they can
play again
score some of the enemies' seeds
Ends when one of the players runs out of seeds
Starting seeding
Choose a cavity on your side (other than the storage)
Collect all N seeds from this cavity
Place 1 seed in the next N cavities
Sowing is done in a counterclockwise direction
Keep on seeding
If the next cavity is your store, sow also
If seeds still remain, continue on the opponent's side
If it hits the opponent's warehouse, don't sow
But then continue on your side, if you still have seeds
Last seed on storage
Plays again if the last seed sown is in your own storage
Sowing now empty cavity, the last seed drops into the warehouse and returns to play
Last seed on empty cavity
If the last seed lands in your empty cavity, transfer the seeds to your storage
in the opposite cavity, of the opponent
just like that last seed sown
Finish game
Game ends when one of the players cannot play because all his cavities are empty
The other player collects seeds in their cavities that are placed in your storage
Wins whoever's storage has more seeds

About Us

The mancala games are a family of two-player turn-based strategy board games played with small stones, beans, or seeds and rows of holes or pits in the earth, a board or other playing surface. The objective is usually to capture all or some set of the opponent's pieces.

This online version of the Game was Developed as a project of the first Semester, last year form Computer Engineering at FEUP. The Mancala Game was proposed by the curricular unit LTW (Languages and Web Technologies).
